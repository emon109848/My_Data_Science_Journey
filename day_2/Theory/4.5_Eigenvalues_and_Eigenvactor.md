## 1Ô∏è‚É£ Definition

* For square matrix (A), **eigenvector** (v) and **eigenvalue** \(\lambda\) satisfy:

\[
A v = \lambda v
\]

* **Interpretation:** Applying transformation (A) to (v) **scales it** by \(\lambda\) but does **not change its direction**.

---

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ Characteristic Equation

* Solve for \(\lambda\) using:

\[
\det(A - \lambda I) = 0
\]

* Gives **eigenvalues**; corresponding vectors ‚Üí **eigenvectors**

---

## 3Ô∏è‚É£ NumPy Syntax

```python
import numpy as np

A = np.array([[2,0],[0,3]])
eigenvalues, eigenvectors = np.linalg.eig(A)

print("Eigenvalues:", eigenvalues)
print("Eigenvectors:\n", eigenvectors)
```

* `np.linalg.eig(A)` ‚Üí returns `(eigenvalues, eigenvectors)`
* Eigenvectors are **columns of the returned matrix**

---

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ Why Important in ML

1. **Dimensionality Reduction**

   * PCA ‚Üí find directions (principal components) where data varies most
   * Eigenvectors ‚Üí directions of variance
   * Eigenvalues ‚Üí magnitude of variance

2. **Feature Engineering / Whitening**

   * Remove correlated features
   * Normalize data along principal axes

3. **Stability Analysis / Control Systems**

   * Eigenvalues determine system stability in dynamic systems

| **PCA**                   | Reduce high-dimensional dataset to top-k components |

## 6Ô∏è‚É£ Quick Tips

* Eigenvectors can be scaled ‚Üí usually **normalized**
* Check `np.allclose(A @ v[:,i], eigenvalues[i]*v[:,i])` to verify
* Only square matrices have eigenvalues/eigenvectors
* Complex eigenvalues possible ‚Üí `np.linalg.eig` handles them
